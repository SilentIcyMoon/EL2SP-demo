Pretraining and Fine-tuning Techniques for Electrolaryngeal Speech Enhancement Based on Sequence-to-sequence Voice Conversion

Authors: Ding Ma, Lester Phillip Violeta, Kazuhiro Kobayashi, Tomoki Toda
Comments: Submitted to IEEE/ACM Transactions on Audio, Speech and Language Processing (TASLP).

Abstract: Sequence-to-sequence (seq2seq) voice conversion (VC) models are highly adept at converting speech with input and output sequences of differing lengths, thereby showing great potential for electrolaryngeal (EL) speech to normal speech converison (EL2SP). However, EL2SP with seq2seq VC requires a large amount of parallel source EL and target normal data for training, otherwise the performance is significantly degraded. While one possible approach to addressing this issue is transfer learning, the substantial differences in acoustic features between EL and normal speech domains make it difficult to directly transfer knowledge from larger-scale normal corpora to the task of EL2SP. To tackle the issues, we present novel methods with muti-stage pretraining and fine-tuning techniques to enhance the pretrained seq2seq VC, as opposed to a common "pretraining-fine-tuning" paradigm. First, we initiate an encoder adaptation training stage with a large amount of imperfect synthetic data and original EL speech to reconcile the general domain knowledge with the specific knowledge required for EL2SP. Then we combine imperfect parallel synthetic data of EL and normal speech with the original dataset into VC training. Finally, the original parallel dataset is used exclusively for the final fine-tuning stage. We study the effectiveness of proposed techniques and demonstrate that the methods lead to a progressive improvement for the performance of EL2SP based on seq2seq VC.

Main concept

Dataset

We recorded three speech sets: RealEL, which is an EL corpus recorded from a laryngectomee; SimuEL and NormSP, which are a simulated EL corpus and a normal corpus recorded from a healthy speaker.

Two experimental cases were conducted to evaluate proposed methods for EL2SP

  • Case 1: SimuEL to NormSP.
  • Case 2: RealEL to NormSP.

Systems

  • EL speech, Normal speech: Original source EL and target normal speech as the reference.
  • Baseline: The Transformer-based model first conducted VTN pretraining then directly fine-tuned with the original EL2SP datasets.
  • Proposed 1: The proposed method first used VTN pretraining then conducted two-stage fine-tuning with the synthetic data and original EL2SP dataset.
    • Stage I of proposed 1: Conversion from first-stage fine-tuning of proposed 1.
    • Stage II of proposed 1: Conversion from final-stage fine-tuning of proposed 1.
  • Proposed 2: The proposed method exploited all pretraining and fine-tuning settings including VTN pretraining, EL adaptaion training and two-stage fine-tuning.
    • Stage I of proposed 2: Conversion from first-stage fine-tuning of proposed 2.
    • Stage II of proposed 2: Conversion from final-stage fine-tuning of proposed 2.
